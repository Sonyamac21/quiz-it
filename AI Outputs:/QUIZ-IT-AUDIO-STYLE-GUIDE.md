# QUIZ-IT AUDIO STYLE GUIDE

**The complete sound identity**
Quiz-It · Powered by Mac Entertainment · by Sonya Mac
Version 1.0 · July 2026 · Audio Director's specification, governed by the Experience Bible §4 and the Live Show Spec

**Reading this guide:** every sound is specified by Purpose · Emotion · Duration · Intensity (1–10 against the night's loudest moment, the Coronation = 10) · Layering (what it's built from and what may play beneath it). Two laws from the Bible govern everything: **the host is the soundtrack** (nothing plays over the mic uninvited) and **audio marks changes of state, never states**.

---

# 1. MUSICAL STYLE — THE QUIZ-IT SOUND

One sentence: **broadcast-orchestral spine, modern electronic muscle.** Low strings and brass for weight and ceremony (the game-show inheritance), analogue-warm synth pulses and processed percussion for modernity (the F1/esports inheritance), and one signature instrument that owns the brand: a **deep dual-thump "heartbeat"** — part kick drum, part sub-bass string hit — that appears in the sonic logo, the countdown, the wheel, and the podium. If the room hears two thumps, it's Quiz-It.

Style boundaries: no comedy instruments except the sanctioned trombone pad; no genre needle-drops (the show's music is *score*, not playlist — the victory-song library is the deliberate exception); no vocals anywhere in the score; nothing that would clash with a venue's own atmosphere at pre-show. Tonally everything sits dark, warm and confident — the audio equivalent of `stage/deep` with purple bloom.

## The sonic logo

- **Purpose:** own three seconds of sound so completely that the brand is audible from the next room.
- **Structure:** two heartbeats → a bright rising synth-brass flare → a purple shimmer tail. (Dark-dark-LIGHT: the brand's stage-then-ignition story in audio.)
- **Emotion:** arrival; "the show is here."
- **Duration:** 2.8s. **Intensity:** 8.
- **Layering:** self-contained; nothing beneath it. Appears at Ignition and the Coronation only — twice a night keeps it sacred.

# 2. UI & FEEDBACK SOUNDS

Handset UI is silent by design 🔒 (haptics carry feedback — Handset A4). The sounds below exist on the **Display and host-triggered contexts only.**

**The deal** (question card arrives)
- Purpose: the room's learned "look up" cue. · Emotion: readiness. · Duration: 0.3s. · Intensity: 3.
- Layering: a crisp card-slide over a soft low thud; plays over silence.

**The flip** (question revealed)
- Purpose: mark the exact moment reading begins. · Emotion: clarity. · Duration: 0.25s. · Intensity: 3.
- Layering: single clean percussive note with a short air tail; tension bed enters after it, never under it.

**Host console sounds:** none. Mission Control is silent — a console that beeps at a performer mid-sentence is sabotage. (Pads and cues are outputs to the room, not console feedback.)

**Button/pad one-shots (the host's instrument):** Airhorn (1.5s, intensity 7), Crowd Cheer (3s, 6), Applause (4s, 5), Sad Trombone (2s, 4), Round Sting (2s, 5), Heartbeat (loopable, 4). Purpose: comedy and punctuation *timed by a human* — the host's timing is the joke; the guide's only rule is that pads duck everything else while firing.

# 3. ANSWER SOUNDS

**Correct — "the crack"**
- Purpose: detonate the tension the silence built; trigger the room's cheer.
- Emotion: release, triumph.
- Duration: 0.6s strike + 1.2s shimmer tail.
- Intensity: 6 (it repeats 30+ times a night; a 9 here would be exhausting by Round 2).
- Layering: one percussive hit (low thud + bright transient) with a green-feeling shimmer tail (high, glassy, major). Built in 3 variation takes, randomly drawn, identical in character. Plays over dead silence — the engineered silence before it is half the sound.

**Incorrect (team-facing contexts, e.g. Hard Deck bust)**
- Purpose: land the truth kindly; give the room permission for the sympathetic "ooof."
- Emotion: dignified fall.
- Duration: 0.8s. · Intensity: 4.
- Layering: a single descending two-note figure, warm and low — never a buzzer, never a klaxon, never comedic. (The standard answer reveal has no "wrong" sound at all: the crack plays for the correct answer; wrongness is visual dimming. Sixty group-failure noises a night would poison the room.)

---

# 4. THE COUNTDOWN — "THE CLOSE"

- **Purpose:** the most recognisable five seconds in quiz entertainment; the room's shared metronome.
- **Structure:** the bed *cuts dead* (the cut is part of the sound), half-beat of silence, then the heartbeat — one dual-thump per second, five times, tempo locked (never accelerating — dread is relentless, not frantic), each thump slightly louder and darker. At zero: the **vault seal** — a heavy metallic shut with sub drop — then total silence.
- **Emotion:** unison adrenaline → held breath.
- **Duration:** 5s + 0.8s seal. · **Intensity:** thumps 5→7; seal 8; the silence after is the loudest thing in the sequence.
- **Layering:** heartbeat + a rising air/riser under thumps 3–5 (felt, not heard). Nothing else. Phones Pulse in sync (silent, haptic).
- **Escalation:** final question of the night — tempo −10%, sub deepened, riser doubled. Same language, higher gear.

---

# 5. THE REVEAL SILENCE

Listed as a sound because it is one, and the most expensive in the show:

- **Purpose:** hand the room to the host; make the crack land.
- **Emotion:** leaning-in suspense.
- **Duration:** host-owned (2s–20s+).
- **Intensity:** 0 by definition — and the guide's hardest rule: *nothing* may sound during a host hold. No shimmer loops, no "suspense drones" unless host-triggered. Empty air with a human voice in it is the format.

# 6. SET-PIECE SCORES

**Spin to Win — "arena glam"**
- Signature (title ignition): bright metallic fanfare, glitter transients over the heartbeat spine. 3s, intensity 7. The mood-shift is instant — regulars' heads turn on the first note.
- The build/summons bed: shimmering pulse, mid-tempo, cocktail-confident. Loopable, intensity 4, ducks under host.
- The charge: rising mechanical whine + accelerating tick, mapped live to the team's held press (the room hears their thumb). Up to 3s, intensity climbs 4→7.
- Reel landings: three heavy mechanical *chunks*, each with sub weight (phones strike in sync). 0.3s each, intensity 6, with the pre-third-reel silence written into the cue.
- Win sting: rising three-note fanfare into shimmer rain, 2.5s, intensity 8. Miss sting: warm brass shrug, two notes, kind, 1.5s, intensity 4.

**The Hard Deck — "the dark score"**
- Signature (title): low string pulse + heartbeat, a semitone darker than anything else in the show. 3s, intensity 6. Stomachs should drop pleasantly.
- Wheel bed: ticking ratchet over low strings, decelerating honestly with the drum; phones pulse with each tick. 8–12s, intensity 5 rising to 6 at the stop.
- The climb bed: a pulse that gains one layer per rung (5→10→20→40 = one added instrument each) — the room *hears* the stakes stack. Loopable per rung, intensity 4–6, ducks fully under host.
- Card flip-strike: the show's heaviest single hit outside the finale — low door-slam + snap. 0.4s, intensity 7, felt on every phone.
- Stick or Gamble: everything strips to a single held pulse (one low note, one heartbeat every 2s). Unbounded, intensity 3 — the emptiness is what lets the crowd's chant become the music.
- Bank: ascending resolve into warm brass, 2.5s, intensity 8. Bust: single falling note + sub drop into silence, 1.5s, intensity 5, then the respect-stamp's soft tick.

**Leaderboard — "the race package"**
- Build bed: propulsive broadcast percussion, bright and short-phrased. ≤45s cap (drama never costs pace), intensity 5.
- Row ticks: one per landing row, 0.1s, intensity 3. Overtake: rising whoosh-and-lock, 1s, intensity 5.
- Top-three notes: three ascending cues, the first-place landing a clean hit. 0.5s each, intensity 6.

**Podium — "the ascent"**
- The gathering: everything out except the heartbeat at half-tempo. Intensity 2. The emptiest the show ever sounds.
- Pillar rises: one rising orchestral note per pillar (bronze warm, silver cooler and *unresolved* — the second-place note deliberately doesn't cadence until the name lands). 3s each, intensity 5→6.
- The empty gold pillar: heartbeat only. However long the host dares. Intensity 2. This near-silence is the finale's fuse.

**Winner — "the coronation"**
- The detonation: sonic logo at full scale straight into the victory song. Intensity 10 — the night's single loudest moment, spent once.
- The victory song: from the winners' library — the sanctioned needle-drop, their trophy in music. Rides 60–90s at intensity 8, then drops to a warm bed under the host for the photo ritual (intensity 4), then hands the air back to the venue.
- The bow: no music. The show's last audio act is getting quieter than the room — grace, scored as silence.

# 7. AMBIENCE & MUSIC BEDS

- **Pre-show bed** (Phases 2–3): warm, mid-tempo, rising in two steps at T-10/T-5; thins and cuts at T-0:10. Intensity 3–4. The only Quiz-It audio that shares air with a venue's atmosphere.
- **Question tension bed:** low pulse, felt-not-heard, enters after the flip, cut dead at T-5s. Loopable, intensity 3. Three interchangeable variants rotated by round so ninety minutes never rides one loop.
- **Between-questions ambience:** near-nothing — a faint air with occasional purple shimmer, intensity 1. The show's resting breath.
- **Interval:** system silent. The venue's air is the interval's sound.
- Bed law: beds never carry melody (melody demands attention; beds *hold* it), and every bed ducks.

---

# 8. TRANSITIONS, DUCKING, CROWD

**Transitions:** audio state changes are cuts or fast ducks (≤200ms), never slow crossfades — the sonic equivalent of snap-and-settle. The *cut to silence* is a named tool (the Close, the reveal, the gathering) and is always intentional, total, and clean.

**Ducking:** the mic is the master bus. Voice-activated duck: beds −12dB within 100ms of the host speaking, restore over 1.5s after they stop (fast down, lazy up — broadcast standard). Stings and one-shots do not duck for the mic (they're punctuation the host times); the host's DUCK latch and KILL override everything.

**Crowd interaction — designing for noise the system doesn't make:** the guide treats the crowd as the show's top layer. Sounds are engineered to *cue* crowd noise, then get out of its way: the crack's tail is short so the cheer owns the air; Stick-or-Gamble strips to a pulse so the chant becomes the score; the countdown's fixed tempo is chantable by design ("FIVE! FOUR!…"); the applause pad exists to *seed* real applause, not replace it (fire it under the third-place reveal and the room takes over). The mix always leaves the middle frequencies — where human voices live — uncrowded.

**Silence, the complete map:** the five engineered silences are the identity as much as any sting — pre-heartbeat black (Ignition), post-seal (the Close), the reveal hold, the empty gold pillar, and the bow. Rule: each is total (no room tone from the system, no residue), each is *entered by a cut*, and each ends with the night's next-most-important sound. A product that is never silent has no dynamic range; Quiz-It's range is what makes intensity 10 possible.

---

# 9. PRODUCTION STANDARDS

Variation pools for every ≥10×/night sound (3+ takes, random draw). Loudness discipline: all cues mastered to a shared reference so the intensity scale above is real; nothing clips a venue PA driven hot. Frequency hygiene: heartbeat and subs own the low end — nothing else lives there; voice band kept clear. Every cue works at low volume (small venues run quiet: cues carry meaning in rhythm and pitch, not just level) and the show remains fully legible muted 🔒 — audio is amplification, never information.
